Nuclear equations — what changes:

  • Alpha decay: mass number decreases by 4; atomic number decreases by 2. A helium-4 nucleus is released.
  • Beta-minus decay: mass number stays the same; atomic number increases by 1. A neutron turns into a proton and an electron is emitted.
  • Gamma decay: no change to mass number or atomic number. Energy is released as a photon.

Half-life: the time it takes for the number of radioactive nuclei in a sample to halve, or equivalently for the count rate to halve.

Uses of radiation: smoke detectors (Am-241, alpha), medical tracers (gamma — detected outside the body), cancer radiotherapy (gamma or beta), sterilising medical equipment (gamma), thickness gauges in industry (beta).

Easy marks to lose: Alpha is the most ionising and least penetrating. Gamma is the least ionising and most penetrating. Students often get this backwards.

Half-Life Calculation — Exam-Style Question
3 mins
▼

Show your working in full. The method marks are worth as much as the final answer.

Exam Question
A radioactive sample has an initial count rate of 800 counts per minute. After 60 years the count rate has fallen to 100 counts per minute. Calculate the half-life of the sample. Show all your working. [3 marks]

Step 1: Work out how many times the count rate has halved.

800 → 400 → 200 → 100

The count rate has halved three times.

Step 2: Divide the total time by the number of half-lives.

Half-life = 60 years ÷ 3 = 20 years

Marks: 1 mark for identifying 3 half-lives; 1 mark for dividing 60 by 3; 1 mark for correct answer with unit.

Second Question
A radioactive isotope has a half-life of 8 days. A hospital receives a sample with an activity of 3200 Bq. What will the activity be after 32 days? [2 marks]

Step 1: Number of half-lives = 32 ÷ 8 = 4 half-lives.

Step 2: 3200 → 1600 → 800 → 400 → 200 Bq

This is a real exam favourite because it tests whether you know to divide the time by the half-life before halving.

Central Nervous System (CNS)

Brain + spinal cord. Processes information and coordinates responses.

Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

All nerves outside the CNS. Carries signals to and from the CNS.

Three types of neuron:

NeuronFunctionLocation
Sensory Carries impulses FROM receptors TO the CNS PNS
Relay Passes impulses within the CNS; links sensory to motor CNS (brain and spinal cord)
Motor Carries impulses FROM the CNS TO effectors (muscles/glands) PNS

Why does a reflex arc bypass the brain? Speed. The relay neuron is in the spinal cord, not the brain, so the signal does not have to travel all the way to the brain and back. This makes reflex responses much faster than conscious responses.

Synapses: the gap between two neurons. When an electrical impulse reaches the end of a neuron, the presynaptic terminal releases neurotransmitter molecules into the synaptic cleft. These diffuse across and bind to receptors on the postsynaptic membrane, triggering a new electrical impulse. Signals can only travel in one direction across a synapse.

Brain regions (required for Higher):

  • Cerebral cortex: consciousness, memory, language, personality, voluntary movement
  • Cerebellum: coordination, balance, fine motor control
  • Medulla oblongata: automatic functions — heart rate, breathing rate

Exam-Style Questions — Nervous System
3 mins
▼
Question A — 3 marks
A student touches a hot plate and immediately pulls their hand away before they feel pain. Explain why the student pulls their hand away before they feel pain. [3 marks]

This is a reflex action. The impulse travels from the receptor in the hand along the sensory neuron to the spinal cord (relay neuron), then along the motor neuron to the effector (muscle), causing the hand to be pulled away. This pathway does not involve the brain, so the response happens before the signal reaches the brain to be consciously felt as pain. [1 mark: identifies reflex arc; 1 mark: explains route through spinal cord not brain; 1 mark: explains this is faster than a conscious response]

Question B — 2 marks
Explain why signals can only travel in one direction across a synapse. [2 marks]

Neurotransmitters are only released from the presynaptic terminal (one side). Receptors for neurotransmitters are only present on the postsynaptic membrane (the other side). Therefore the signal can only travel from one neuron to the next, not backwards.

Photosynthesis

5
Equation, Limiting Factors & Uses of Glucose
5 mins: read then recall
▼
PHOTOSYNTHESIS EQUATION
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂   (requires light energy)

Word equation: carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen (using light energy)

Photosynthesis takes place in the chloroplasts, specifically in the thylakoid membranes (light-dependent stage) and the stroma (light-independent stage / Calvin cycle).

The three limiting factors — learn the graph shape for each:

Limiting FactorEffect on rateExam detail
Light intensity As light increases, rate increases (up to a point, then levels off) At low light, light is the limiting factor. When the graph flattens, something else is limiting.
CO₂ concentration As CO₂ increases, rate increases (up to a point) CO₂ is a raw material for photosynthesis. Without enough of it, rate cannot increase.
Temperature Rate increases up to the optimum (~25–35°C); falls sharply above optimum Enzymes catalyse photosynthesis. Above optimum the enzymes denature. This is not just "slow down" — it is permanent denaturation.

What does the plant do with the glucose?

  • Respiration — releases energy for the plant's own processes
  • Starch — insoluble storage molecule (converted from glucose)
  • Cellulose — used to make cell walls
  • Proteins — glucose + nitrate ions from the soil → amino acids → proteins
  • Fats and oils — energy storage in seeds

For Higher tier — the two stages:

Light-Dependent (thylakoid membranes)
  • Light energy splits water (photolysis): 2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻
  • ATP is made
  • Oxygen is released as a by-product
  • NADPH is produced
Light-Independent / Calvin Cycle (stroma)
  • CO₂ is fixed (combined with RuBP using enzyme RuBisCO)
  • ATP and NADPH from stage 1 are used
  • Glucose (GP → GALP → glucose) is produced
  • RuBP is regenerated
Examiner's favourite mistake: Students say temperature just "slows down" photosynthesis above the optimum. The correct answer is that the enzymes involved (including RuBisCO) are denatured above the optimum temperature — their active site permanently changes shape. This is worth an extra mark.

Exam-Style Questions — Photosynthesis
3 mins
▼
Question A — 3 marks
A scientist measures the rate of photosynthesis in a plant at increasing light intensities. The rate increases at first, then levels off, even though light intensity continues to increase. Explain why the rate levels off. [3 marks]

When the graph levels off, light is no longer the limiting factor. Another factor — either CO₂ concentration or temperature — has become limiting. Even if light is increased further, the rate cannot increase because there is insufficient CO₂ for the Calvin cycle (or the enzymes are not working fast enough at the current temperature). [1 mark: identifies that another factor is now limiting; 1 mark: names a specific factor; 1 mark: explains mechanism, e.g. insufficient CO₂ for the light-independent reactions]

Question B — 2 marks
Explain why plants need nitrate ions as well as glucose. [2 marks]

Glucose only contains carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. Proteins also require nitrogen. Plants absorb nitrate ions from the soil and combine them with glucose derivatives to produce amino acids, which are then assembled into proteins. Without nitrate ions, plants cannot make proteins and will show stunted growth.

Single Most Important Habit Across All Three Topics

When an exam question asks you to "explain" something in science, the answer requires a mechanism, not just a description. "The rate goes down because it is too hot" is worth one mark. "The enzymes are denatured above the optimum temperature, permanently changing the shape of the active site so substrates can no longer bind" is worth two or three. In every explain question, ask yourself: what is physically or chemically happening at the molecular or cellular level? Write that.
